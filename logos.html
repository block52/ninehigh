Logo Concepts

Five logo directions for 9 High Studios. Each concept captures a different facet of the brand — poker heritage, technology, trust, and craft.

Concept 01

The Diamond Nine

A bold "9" set inside a diamond suit shape. The diamond is the most geometric of the four suits, conveying precision and technology. The serif "9" adds a classic poker feel while the clean diamond frame keeps it modern.

Classic Geometric Iconic
Concept 02

The Card Edge

Inspired by the corner of an actual playing card showing the nine of diamonds. Instantly recognisable to poker players, this concept leans into the heritage of the game while the typographic lockup beside it keeps it professional. Works brilliantly as a favicon and app icon.

Heritage Recognisable App-Ready
Concept 03

The Network Node

Matches the existing network/node aesthetic already used on the homepage. The "9" sits at the centre of a decentralised network, with card suit symbols at each satellite node. Communicates technology, connectivity, and the multiplayer nature of poker. This concept has the strongest visual continuity with the current site.

Tech-Forward Decentralised On-Brand
Concept 04

The Typographic Mark

Pure typography — just "9H" (nine-high, the hand) in a bold serif with contrasting weights. The red 9 against the light H creates a striking monogram that works at any size. Extremely versatile as an app icon, watermark, or embroidered mark. Understated elegance for a premium brand.

Minimal Versatile Premium
Concept 05

The Chip

A poker chip emblem with the "9" at its centre, ringed by the four card suits. The chip notches around the edge are instantly recognisable to anyone who's played live poker. Communicates authority, trust, and the physical feel of the game — perfect for a company building the digital equivalent.

Emblem Authoritative Tactile
Boutique Collection

Sharper edges. Finer details. These five concepts push into premium territory — think fashion house, think fine spirits, think the private room at the back of the casino.

Concept 06

The Engraved Seal

Inspired by banknote engraving and luxury spirit labels. Concentric fine-line rings, crosshatch fill, and gold tones give this a currency-grade feel. The curved text reads like a wax seal or coin — "NINE HIGH STUDIOS / EST. MMXXV". This says "we don't cut corners".

Engraved Currency-Grade Prestigious
Concept 07

The Negative Space Blade

An outlined "9" sits inside a sharp, slightly tapered trapezoid — like a blade or a monolith. The number is rendered as a stroke rather than a fill, giving it a technical-drawing precision. A diagonal slash cuts across for edge. The ultra-light "NINE HIGH" text below feels haute couture. Red-to-gold gradient on the frame signals premium.

Sharp Architectural Haute
Concept 08

The Crown

Five diamond suit shapes arranged as a crown above the "9". The centre diamond rises tallest — forming the peak. It's regal without being gaudy, like the logo on a VIP poker room door. The red diamond accent below the nine adds the signature brand colour. Says "we run the table".

Regal VIP Distinctive
Concept 09

The Art Deco Split

The "9" is split down the centre — the left half is solid red, the right half is rendered as a gold outline with horizontal engraving lines showing through. Framed in an Art Deco rectangle with corner ornaments. This is the kind of logo you'd see etched into frosted glass at a 1920s poker parlour. Dual nature: digital precision meets handcrafted tradition.

Art Deco Engraved Dual Nature
Concept 10

The Monogram Crest

A classic heraldic shield containing an interlocking "NH" monogram — the N in gold, the H in red, overlapping at the centre. A small "9" sits at the top of the shield like a family crest. Four card suits mark the compass points. "EST. MMXXV" at the base. This is fashion-house branding applied to poker — Hermès meets the high-stakes room. The crest works beautifully foil-stamped on cards, embossed on leather, or watermarked on chips.

Heraldic Fashion-House Foil-Ready
